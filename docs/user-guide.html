User Guide — how to build and share the office calendar

This guide walks you through setting up a month, generating the staff calendar, adjusting it, and sharing it. The office runs Monday–Friday only.

1. Two ways to use the app

RoleWhat they can do
EditorSet monthly patterns, generate the calendar, drag and edit assignments, and manage the staff roster. There is one editor.
ViewerSee the live calendar, move between months, and export to Excel — but cannot change anything. No sign-in needed.

A chip in the top-right corner shows your mode: a green Editor chip or a grey Viewer chip.

The basic flow

  • 1. In Monthly Setup, enter who works which days, where, plus time off and holidays.
  • 2. Click Generate month to auto-build the calendar.
  • 3. Drag and click tiles to fine-tune, watching the warning flags.
  • 4. Export to Excel to print or email it.

2. Signing in (Editor)

  • Click Sign in at the top-right of the calendar.
  • Enter your username and password. You only type the username — the rest of the email address is added for you automatically.
  • Once signed in, the editing buttons (Roster, Monthly setup, Generate month) appear and the chip turns green.

Click Sign out anytime to return to read-only view. Viewers don't need to sign in at all — they just open the site.

3. Reading the calendar

The calendar shows one week per row, scrolling down through the month. Each day is a column, and staff are grouped into rows by role.

Location colors

Every tile is colored by where that person is that day (the color key is in the top bar):

  • Kona (purple)
  • Waimea (blue)
  • Remote (green)
  • Off / R-O (grey) — not working, or requested off

Tile badges

  • MOD — Manager on Duty that day.
  • 📦 — handling Shipping.
  • 📣 — assigned to Social Media.
  • 🔄 Name(s) — who this person covers (an absent provider, or a PCC's targets).
  • 🖌️ text — a custom note was added for that day.

Medical Assistants & holidays

  • MAs appear nested under their provider — each provider has up to two MA slots.
  • A holiday shows as a greyed-out column with a Holiday tag and no staff.
  • If a day has a problem, an amber ⚠ warning chip appears in its header (see section 7).

4. Monthly Setup (Editor)

Click Monthly setup, then use the ‹ Month › arrows to pick the month you're editing.

Holidays

The amber Holidays box at the top takes a day list like 1, 4-5. Any weekday you list becomes office closed — no staff, greyed out. Leave it blank for none.

Each person's row

  • Mon–Fri columns — set the location for each weekday: — (not working), Kona, Waimea, Remote, or Kona / Waimea (alternating) which switches week to week.
  • Requested off — time-off days as ranges, e.g. 1-3, 8-11.
  • Defaults & ranks — extra options depending on role:
    • Providers: Priority # (fill order), 2 MAs (give this provider a second MA), Coverage (needs coverage when out / can cover others).
    • MAs & support staff: a default Provider/target pairing, plus MOD # and 📦 # ranks (lower number = picked first).

Carry forward & save

  • Carry forward copies the previous month's weekday patterns, locations, and ranks into this month (all still editable). Time off does not carry over — re-enter it each month.
  • Save all stores everything for the month.
Tip: Enter the first month by hand. After that, Carry forward saves most of the typing each month.

5. Generating the month

On the calendar, with the right month selected, click Generate month. The app fills in every weekday automatically:

  • Sets each person's attendance and location (holidays are skipped).
  • Picks one Manager on Duty per day.
  • Assigns provider coverage for anyone out who needs it.
  • Gives every working provider an MA (and a second to "2 MAs" providers).
  • Assigns PCCs / concierge to cover providers, estheticians, and wellness.
  • Sets shipping.
Important: Generating replaces the whole month. It rebuilds every day from your setup, so any by-hand edits for that month are cleared. Set up first, generate once, then fine-tune by hand.

6. Adjusting the calendar by hand (Editor)

After generating, you can change anything. The warnings update instantly as you go.

Drag an MA to another provider

Grab a Medical Assistant tile and drop it onto a different provider card to reassign them.

Click any tile to open the editor

Click a tile to open the side panel, where you can set:

  • Location (Kona / Waimea / Remote / Off)
  • Manager on Duty toggle
  • Assigned provider (for MAs)
  • Coverage — which absent providers this provider covers
  • PCC covers — which targets a PCC/concierge coordinates
  • Shipping 📦 and Social Media 📣 toggles
  • Custom note — free text shown on the tile

Click Save to apply or Cancel to discard.

7. Warnings

Warnings flag likely problems but never block you. They update live, and you can dismiss one by clicking the × next to it.

WarningMeaning
No MODNo Manager on Duty is set that day.
Provider has 0 MAsA working provider has no MA.
Provider has too many MAsA provider has more than 2 MAs.
Out provider has no coverageAn absent provider who needs coverage has nobody covering.
MA location mismatchAn MA is under a provider at a different location.
Target has no PCC coverageA provider/esthetician/wellness has no same-location PCC.

Fix a warning by editing the relevant tiles, or dismiss it if it's acceptable.

8. Managing the roster (Editor)

Click Roster to manage staff.

  • Add a person — type a name, pick a role, click + Add person.
  • Deactivate — removes someone from future schedules but keeps their history.
  • Show inactive — toggle to reveal deactivated people. For each, you can:
    • Reactivate — bring them back into scheduling, or
    • Delete — permanently remove the person and all of their history (you'll be asked to confirm; this can't be undone).
Tip: Deactivate is the safe, reversible choice. Only Delete when you're sure you'll never need that person's past schedule.

9. Exporting to Excel

Click Export Excel (available to everyone, including Viewers) to download a spreadsheet of the current month, laid out like the calendar with color-coded locations and all the badges. Handy for printing or emailing.

10. Quick tips

  • See “No schedule generated yet”? As Editor, set up the month and click Generate month.
  • Everyone showing as Off? That month's setup is empty — fill it in, save, then generate.
  • Use the ‹ › arrows in the top bar to move between months.
  • Lost by-hand edits usually mean someone re-ran Generate month — it rebuilds the whole month.
